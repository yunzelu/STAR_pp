Standing/Walking; Head: Remains vertically aligned and facing forward, with slight vertical oscillation during movement; Hand: Remains relaxed at the sides, either static or swinging in opposition to leg movement; Arm: Hangs naturally, either extended downwards statically or swinging rhythmically forward and backward from the shoulder; Hip: Remains upright, either stable or rotating slightly to support alternating weight transfer; Leg: Extended to support weight, either static or alternating between a flexed swing phase and an extended stance phase; Foot: Planted firmly on the ground, either static or alternating between heel strike, rolling midfoot, and toe push-off.
Falling; Head: Flexes rapidly forward or extends back as the body loses vertical alignment; Hand: Extends and reaches out rapidly to break the fall or grab an object; Arm: Extends forcefully forward or to the sides in an attempt to catch the body weight; Hip: Drops quickly towards the ground as the center of gravity moves outside the base of support; Leg: Flexes uncontrollably or locks up, unable to maintain balance; Foot: Leaves contact with the ground or twists as balance is lost.
Transition-LayFloor-to-Stand; Head: Lifts off the floor, moving forward and upward to align vertically over the torso; Hand: Presses flat against the floor to bear weight, then releases as the body rises; Arm: Bends to position hands on the floor, then forcefully extends to push the upper body upward; Hip: Flexes to shift the center of mass over the feet, then extends to straighten the torso into a vertical stance; Leg: Flexes at the knees to bring the feet under the body, then extends to push the body upward; Foot: Transitions from resting to planting firmly on the floor, pushing down to drive the body up.
Transition-Stand-to-Sit; Head: Remains mostly stable, may look down towards the seat; Hand: May reach out to steady the body or rest on the legs/seat; Arm: May extend forward to counterbalance or support the body weight on the chair; Hip: Flexes (bends) as the body lowers down and backward towards the seat; Leg: Flexes (knees bend) to allow the body to lower smoothly; Foot: Remains planted on the ground, moves backward slightly as the body lowers.
Transition-Sit-to-LayBed; Head: Moves backward and downward until resting flat on the surface; Hand: Presses against the bed to support the body weight, then relaxes; Arm: Flexes to control the descent of the torso, then relaxes; Hip: Extends as the torso reclines backward from a vertical to a horizontal position; Leg: Lifts and extends to rest horizontally on the bed; Foot: Lifts off the ground and rests on the bed, minimal movement once laying.
Transition-LayBed-to-Sit; Head: Lifts off the bed, moving forward and upward to align vertically over the torso; Hand: Presses flat against the bed surface to bear weight and assist the lift; Arm: Flexes to position the hands, then extends to push the upper body upward and forward; Hip: Flexes to bring the torso into an upright vertical position relative to the legs; Leg: Swings off the edge of the bed and flexes at the knees to lower toward the floor; Foot: Lowers towards the ground and plants firmly on the floor.
Transition-Sit-to-Stand; Head: Moves forward and up over the feet; Hand: Pushes off the seat or knees for assistance, or remains relaxed; Arm: Pushes down on support (chair, knees) to lift the body; Hip: Extends (straightens) as the body rises vertically; Leg: Extends (knees straighten) to push the body upward; Foot: Pushes down into the ground to drive the body up, planted firmly.
Sitting; Head: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hand: Remains stable, minimal movement; Arm: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hip: Flexed to support the seated torso, remains stable; Leg: Flexed at the knees, remains stable; Foot: Remains stable, minimal movement.
Lying on the Bed; Head: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hand: Remains stable, minimal movement; Arm: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hip: Extended to align the torso and legs horizontally, remains stable; Leg: Extended horizontally, remains stable; Foot: Remains stable, minimal movement.
Lying on the Floor after Falling; Head: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hand: Remains stable, minimal movement; Arm: Remains stable, minimal movement; Hip: Remains stable, minimal movement; Leg: Remains stable, minimal movement; Foot: Remains stable, minimal movement.
